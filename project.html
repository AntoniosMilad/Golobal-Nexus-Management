Lam Data Center Oregon. Portland. USA

In 2019, Google was awarded a $600 million project to expand its data center campus in Oregon, as tech giants and real estate developers rush to build technical systems in preparation for artificial intelligence-fueled growth. The Dalles, a small city about 80 miles east of Portland, approved Google's application to build a fifth data center at its campus along the Columbia River. This was according to a notice from the city's community development department released this week. The project aims to expand the complex that opened in 2006 as Google's first corporate-owned data center. The sprawling campus has grown over the years and is now reported to be equal in size to two football fields. This latest project comes as the once-small data center niche benefits from the demand for computing speed and cloud storage capacity, as well as growth from the artificial intelligence sector.
